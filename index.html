Conventional approach

Conventional piano sight reading approach suggests us to count as follow:

  • What note is this?

  • hm... let's start with the Middle-C

  • ... the one below is a B

  • ... then an A

  • ... and G

  • ... and F

  • ... and E

  • ... finally the D

  • ... where is it on the piano? Here.

  • Counting 8 steps for 1 note?

    In paino playback we are dealing with a stream of notes, we don't have the leisure of seconds to count. But how some of the pianist can sight read a new piece in real time?

Recommended approach

1. Stop counting the lines and spaces.
2. Visualizing the piano.
3. Train your instincts.

  • G-B-D-F-A, therefore a D.

  • ... and the D on the piano looks like this.

  • Result, 3 Steps.

  • TIP#1: You do not need to count to know which line (or space) a note is on (e.g. this one is in the middle). TIP#2: Open and close the card until you see the key even when the card is close. TIP#3: Do you know that both the lines and spaces are repersenting white keys? Each white keys can line up nicely with all the line and space. It's like turning a full piano keyboard 90 degrees counter clockwise.
